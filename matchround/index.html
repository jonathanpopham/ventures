For cohort placement teams

Run your cohort's job placement like the residency match.

One synchronized round. Every grad and employer submits real preferences. A stable match, computed — nobody left refreshing an inbox.

Placement season is weeks of exploding offers, ghosted applications, and grads accepting early out of fear while your best employer partners wait on someone who already signed elsewhere. Your placement rate is your product — and the process is chaos.

1

Both sides rank, once

Grads rank employers; employers rank grads. Real preferences over a bounded set, gathered in one window.

2

The algorithm clears the market

One synchronized round computes a stable assignment: no grad/employer pair exists who'd both rather have each other than what they got. That's a theorem, not a heuristic.

3

You publish results, with receipts

Every placement comes with an auditable explanation. Your placement stats become defensible, not anecdotal.

The same Nobel-recognized algorithm (Gale–Shapley, 1962; Roth's market-design Nobel, 2012) that has matched every U.S. medical resident to their hospital since the 1950s — applied to your cohort.
